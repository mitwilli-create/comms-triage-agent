# Knowledge Base: Touch Level Tier Structure

**Purpose:** Define the three routing tiers, their characteristics, and how to distinguish between them.

---

## Tier Definitions

### LOW TOUCH — Tier 3

**Core definition:** Async revision with rationale. No meeting, strategy, or coordination required.

**What makes something Low Touch:**
- The requester has an existing draft that needs editing
- The audience is internal and familiar
- The timeline is flexible or the task is simple enough for same-day completion
- The cost of an error is low and recoverable
- No strategic decisions needed — only execution

**Typical request types:**
- Email draft review and copy editing
- Announcement optimization
- Template-based communications (status updates, bug comms)
- Revisions to existing materials

**Agent action:** Handles autonomously end-to-end. Turnaround: same-day or next-day.

**Disqualifiers (escalate if any are true):**
- VP+ audience in any capacity
- External-facing component
- Requester asks for strategy guidance
- Confidence score below 70%
- Any [VP_NAME] involvement

---

### MEDIUM TOUCH — Tier 2

**Core definition:** Strategy or playbook creation requiring synthesis and planning.

**What makes something Medium Touch:**
- The requester doesn't have a draft — they need a strategy or plan
- The audience is cross-org
- The deliverable requires research or coordination with 1–2 stakeholders
- Moderate business impact if mishandled

**Typical request types:**
- Comms strategy documents
- Step-by-step playbooks with rationale
- Consultation on approach, channels, or messaging

**Agent action:** Routes to [ESCALATION_OWNER] with complete briefing. Turnaround: 3–5 business days.

---

### HIGH TOUCH — Tier 1

**Core definition:** Full partnership from discovery through execution. VP+ visibility, high stakes.

**What makes something High Touch:**
- VP+ audience in any capacity
- Org-wide or external distribution
- Urgent deadline (<3 days) combined with strategic complexity
- Significant cost of failure
- Any involvement of [VP_NAME] — absolute override, no exceptions

**Agent action:** Routes to [ESCALATION_OWNER] immediately with complete briefing. Turnaround: 1–4 weeks.

---

## Tier Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Low Touch | Medium Touch | High Touch |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Draft exists? | Yes | No | Sometimes |
| Audience scope | Internal/team | Cross-org | Org-wide or VP+ |
| Strategic decisions needed? | No | Yes | Yes |
| [VP_NAME] involved? | No | No | Auto-escalate |
| Cost of failure | Low | Moderate | High |
| Agent acts autonomously? | Yes | No | No |
| Turnaround | Same/next day | 3–5 days | 1–4 weeks |

---

## Default Rules When Uncertain

1. When two factors point to different tiers → Default UP one level
2. When timeline is unknown → Treat as Moderate (Medium signal)
3. When audience size is unknown → Ask before classifying
4. When confidence is below 70% → Escalate regardless of other factors
5. When [VP_NAME] is mentioned anywhere → High Touch, no exceptions
