Organization Values
Mission: “Organize the world’s information and make it universally accessible and useful.”

Ours is not a conventional mission. Few organizations would imagine such a thing, much less pursue it. We have no choice but to match the ambition of our mission with an equal amount of responsibility in carrying it out. To sign up for the mission is to sign up for the responsibility that comes with it (source).
The Three Respects
Our values are how we conduct ourselves in the pursuit of our mission. They are not to be led by some and followed by others; they are to be owned personally by each of us and collectively by all of us. They are at heart, a balance of trust extended and responsibility accepted (source).
1. Respect the user
Users are people. They are family members, friends, and neighbors. Some are relying on our products to build their company, their clinic, or their non-profit. Others just need help finding an address. But every one of them is giving us their trust. It is your responsibility to do right by them (source).

This means:

	•	Focus on the user and all else will follow. It all comes back to this. Improve the lives of users and everything else tends to work out (source).
	•	Not just one user...everyone. A problem isn’t truly solved if it’s only solved for some. Aim for everyone. As long as barriers like complexity, cost, and access exist, there’s still work to do (source).
	•	Give people a voice. And a chance. Build products that create opportunities for people to participate and grow. Their collective impact will be greater than anything we do on our own (source).
	•	Treat every bit of data the way you’d want your data to be treated. Every one of our users is trusting us. Never jeopardize that. Ever (source).
2. Respect the opportunity
Working at the organization comes with tremendous responsibility -- not only to do the right thing, but to accomplish things that matter. The decisions you make have the potential to affect billions of people and each one (big and small) defines what the organization is (source).

This means:

	•	We only succeed when others succeed. Ultimately, we make tools to help others. Small businesses, big enterprises, developers, creators, partners, journalists, teachers, and everyone in between. If they don’t succeed, neither do we (source).
	•	Identify the real problem. Then solve it brilliantly. Chasing symptoms (and competitors) is a waste of time. Use deep insight and technology to solve the problem behind the problem. Then demand great execution and attention to detail (source).
	•	Think big. Act small. Stay humble. Have a healthy disregard for the impossible. That’s how we started. And how we’re at our best. Stay scrappy. Have fun. And never stop thinking like an entrepreneur (source).
3. Respect each other
We have the potential to help billions. But we are no good to anyone if we can’t first be good to each other (source).

This means:

	•	Treat each other with dignity. Every team member is unique. We come from different places. We hold different views. But each of us has earned the opportunity to be here (source).
	•	You can disagree without being disagreeable. Despite any of our differences… we’re all here to accomplish the same mission. Respectfully listen, debate, decide, and then move forward as a team (source).
	•	Ultimately, each other means everyone. team members live in nearly every part of the world. Respect their communities, their institutions, and the planet we all share. It’s everyone’s world. We need to leave it better than we found it (source).
