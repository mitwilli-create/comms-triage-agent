1. Framework Deconstruction
Mission & Core Value
The Unified ROI Framework aims to establish a consistent, VP-reviewed financial framework for the Office of [ORG_UNIT] ([TEAM_NAME]) and Core Finance (source). Its primary goal is to address the lack of a standardized method for evaluating technical initiatives, enabling:

	•	Data-driven prioritization of engineering investments (source).
	•	Standardized decision-making to reduce coordination headwinds (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).
	•	Transparency in communicating costs and benefits to stakeholders (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).
Methodology: NPV vs. ROI
While the initiative is titled "Unified ROI," the financial analysis prioritizes Net Present Value (NPV) and Payback Period (PBP) over simple Return on Investment (ROI) percentages (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).

	•	Why NPV? NPV is considered superior because it accounts for the time value of money (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]). A high ROI achieved over 20 years is less valuable than a lower ROI realized in 2 years; NPV captures this distinction (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).
	•	The Calculation: The framework estimates future cash flows, determines a discount rate (often WACC), and discounts those flows to their present value (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).

5. Impact Synthesis & Case Studies
The framework has demonstrated significant impact across multiple high-priority initiatives, validating its methodology.
Key Success Stories
	•	Wipeout for Spanner:
	•	Context: Assessment of a company-wide mandate for Wipeout adoption.
	•	Impact: Validated a positive expected financial impact with a projected ROI of 800% to 2,400% (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]). The primary driver was Risk Mitigation (regulatory fines) which outweighed labor costs (Unified ROI [[INTERNAL_LINK]]).

	•	Context: Investment in documentation and monitoring policies.
	•	Impact: Implementation of both policies projected a Risk Reduction of $556M in Year 1 and an ROI of 2,400%–4,700% (source). This analysis unblocked investment in LLM-based remediation solutions (source).
	•	Ganpati1 to Ganpati2 Migration:
	•	Context: Justifying migration despite risks.
	•	Impact: Confirmed positive financial impact validated by Core Finance, unblocking faster progress toward security/reliability compliance (DMA 5(2)) (source).

	•	Impact: The ROI analysis led to a pivot from "Asset Valuation" to "Risk Based on access," resulting in a recommendation against a broad mandate in favor of targeted rollouts on High Impact groups (source).
